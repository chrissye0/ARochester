Geva Theater

ABOUT

Founded in 1972, Geva serves up to 160,000 patrons annually, including more than 16,000 students. Geva’s productions are created and rehearsed in and for Rochester. Geva draws upon the talents of the country’s top actors, directors, designers and writers who are shaping the American theatre landscape.

Geva Theatre is an active member of the Rochester community. In addition to serving the community through its productions, Geva Theatre offers a wide variety of educational, outreach, literary and community connectivity programs.

ANECDOTES

Interview with Tracey

Transcript

One of my favorite activities in the Rochester area is checking out the performing arts scene. We’re incredibly lucky to have such beautiful theatres and a wealth of talented performers here in Upstate New York. At least once a year, my best friend and I make it a point to see a show at Geva Theatre in the city. While we often opt for familiar titles, we occasionally take a chance on something lesser-known.

My first experience with Geva was in middle school during a field trip to see their annual production of A Christmas Carol. That visit sparked my love for the theatre. With its intimate lobby, charming bar and snack area, and the warm presence of welcoming docents, Geva makes a great first impression. Despite the stage appearing modest in size, it’s remarkably versatile—and truly, there’s not a bad seat in the house.

During my senior year of undergrad, a friend and I even had season tickets. It gave us a standing outing every couple of months and introduced us to a mix of plays—some familiar, others completely new. A Christmas Carol isn’t part of the season ticket package, but it’s absolutely worth seeing; the dramatization changes each year, keeping the experience fresh.

Geva Theatre is a gem for both Rochester natives and visitors alike—especially college students. Shows typically run from September through June and performances usually span multiple dates, making it easy to find a time that works. It’s totally worth it!
