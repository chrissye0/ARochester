Rochester Institute of Technology

ABOUT

RIT offers a dynamic, interdisciplinary education that empowers students to innovate and lead at the intersection of technology, the arts, and design across our 11 diverse colleges and degree-granting units. With flexible academic pathways including Combined Accelerated Bachelor’s/Master’s Degree, double majors, and specialized minors and immersions, students can tailor their education to meet their unique interests and career goals. Graduates join a global alumni network of 155,000, including CEOs, innovators, and Academy Award winners.

ANECDOTES

Interview with Igor Polotai

Transcript

So Rochester Institute of Technology is certainly a place with a lot of different stories. So I have been working on the RIT Iceberg, which is kind of a collection of different folklores, myths, and stories for the last two years now, so I have a couple of good stories.

The central statue in the middle of campus can control the weather. The best place to view the statue is the president’s office, and so the president uses the Sentinel to make sure that we have nice and sunny days for important events. If you go into the woods right outside Grace Watson Hall, you can go visit the Crack Shack. There’s also a tunnel that goes into the sewers that is also in the woods for some reason. There are many different secret staircases that lead out to weird doors. There are also secret tunnel connections between the dorm side and academic side tunnels. Now, RIT officially says that this is false, but a lot of our alumni I’ve talked to say that there are actually service tunnels that go in between them, so the hunt is still on to actually find them.

And there’s a lot of small stuff that you might see - different stickers all around, QR codes, graffiti, especially in the tunnels of academic side in between the Engineering and Liberal Arts buildings. Those are places where you see a lot of student expression and a lot of very old murals. Probably the oldest murals you can find are going to be in the residential dorm side tunnels. You have to go to the specific storage closet, and if you do, you’ll be able to see murals that were put up around 1970, so that’s about the time RIT came to this campus. So keep your eyes peeled whenever you’re on campus and you never know what you might see.
